Decision Support for Improving the Use of Antimicrobials in Hospitals

The software project described at https://www.guidance.org.au is a web-based decision-support system developed by the Royal Melbourne Hospital (RMH) to improve antimicrobial prescribing and stewardship in hospitals. It provides real-time decision support based on local and national guidelines, integrates with electronic medical records and pharmacy systems, and supports workflows for prescribers, pharmacists, nurses and antimicrobial-stewardship teams.

As a software engineer working on this project, you would contribute to building and maintaining modules that allow clinicians to approve and track antimicrobial use, generate reports on prescribing patterns, and support quality-improvement feedback loops.

Your work would support the hospital’s mission to reduce misuse of broad-spectrum antibiotics and lower antimicrobial resistance. Through this system, RMH has achieved measurable improvements including reductions in broad-spectrum antibiotic use, lower drug expenditure, and declines in rates of certain resistant infections.
